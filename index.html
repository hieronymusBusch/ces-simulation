Economic interpretation

Baseline discrimination. Each skill tier has its own discrimination coefficient: dH for high-skill and dL for low-skill women. Firms act as if female workers cost (1+d)·wF in each tier. In equilibrium, women earn below their MPL — wFH = 1/(1+dH) and wFL = 1/(1+dL) — but employment within each tier is equalized across gender (effective costs are symmetric at 1). The two wage gaps can be calibrated independently.

High-skill mandate. Forcing wFH = wMH raises FH wages by dH×100% and lifts the firm's effective FH cost to (1+dH)·wMH. Firms substitute toward MH at rate σwH. The low-skill nest is unaffected directly: wFL stays at 1/(1+dL) and its effective cost to the firm remains 1, so dL and σwL do not drive the policy comparative statics in this experiment.

The paradox of equal pay. The FH wage bill falls when σwH > 1/dH. With separate tier parameters the threshold is governed entirely by the high-skill tier: higher dH raises the mandated wage but amplifies the employment distortion, while higher σwH accelerates substitution away from FH. These interact multiplicatively, so the paradox is more likely when both are large.

Cross-skill spillover. As cH rises (driven by dH and σwH), the outer nest substitutes toward L at rate σb, mildly lifting ML and FL employment symmetrically. The magnitude depends only on ΔcH and σb — it is independent of dL and σwL in this experiment.

Key comparative statics: dH ↑ → larger FH wage gain but stronger employment loss; paradox deepens. σwH ↑ → more aggressive MH/FH substitution. σb ↑ → larger spillover to low-skill groups. dL → sets FL baseline wage gap only; does not affect policy employment changes here. σwL → inert for this experiment (cL = 2 regardless of σwL when w̃FL = 1); becomes active under a joint mandate on both skill tiers.
Activating σwL and dL: Under the current high-skill-only mandate, FL's effective cost to the firm is (1+dL)·wFL = 1 both before and after the policy (since wFL = 1/(1+dL) is unchanged). To bring σwL into play, extend the mandate to also force wFL = wML. At that point w̃FL rises to (1+dL), cL increases, and the low-skill nest generates an analogous employment response governed by σwL.
